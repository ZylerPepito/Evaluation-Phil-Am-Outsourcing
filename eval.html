Evaluation Form

The employee evaluation form is an important tool for assessing an employee's performance and providing valuable feedback. Completing this form objectively ensures fairness, accuracy, and promotes constructive growth.

1. Knowledge of Duties

Demonstrates a clear understanding and ability to perform the assigned job duties and has in-depth knowledge and technical expertise. Learns and masters applicable new skills and procedures.
This assesses the effective application of the knowledge and skills required by the job being performed, including but not limited to, written and oral communication.

2. Productivity

An acceptable volume of work is completed in a timely manner.
This assesses the volume (i.e., speed and consistency) of output taking into account intervening assignments and/or other factors affecting performance which were outside the employee's control.

3. Quality of Work/Accuracy

Performs a high level of competency, accuracy and thoroughness. Uses initiative and creativity as appropriate in providing service.
This assesses the quality (i.e., substance, content, completeness and accuracy) of output taking into account intervening assignments and/or other factors affecting performance which were outside the employee's control.

4. Attendance

Schedules and uses leave in an appropriate manner that is sensitive to the department and workload priorities. Adheres to work schedule.

5. Punctuality

Adheres to work schedule; reports to work on time; abides by company policy on break periods.

6. Effectiveness/Efficiency

Demonstrates the ability to use time wisely in producing the volume or quantity of work required for the position.

7. Follow Through/Prioritize

Demonstrates good judgment in planning, organizing, and completing work.

8. Interpersonal/Communication Skills

The ability to listen, hear and respond in a sensitive, meaningful way that enhances mutual respect with others as the employee carries out his/her responsibilities.

9. Adaptability/Initiative

Demonstrates the ability to learn new tasks, develop new skills, and to accept new ideas as the work environment changes over time.

10. Flexibility

Demonstrates the ability to accommodate unexpected changes in the work routine.

11. Cooperative/Team Player

Works well and effectively with others; responsive, positive attitude towards work; ability and willingness to work with associates, administrators, and subordinates towards common goals; accommodating and dependable.
